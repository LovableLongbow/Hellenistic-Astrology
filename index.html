Introduction to Hellenistic Astrology

Hellenistic astrology is the earliest systematic form of Western astrology, developed between the 2nd century BCE and the 6th century CE. Drawing from a synthesis of Babylonian astronomical techniques and Greek philosophical frameworks, it represents a structured approach to interpreting celestial phenomena in relation to human life.

This tradition emphasizes concepts such as fate, fortune, and character through defined techniques including the use of whole sign houses, planetary sect, and time-lord systems. This site aims to present the foundational theories and methodologies of Hellenistic astrology within their historical and philosophical context.
